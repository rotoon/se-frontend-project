Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about traveling to Chiang Mai

The best time to visit Chiang Mai is during the cool season from November to February, when temperatures are comfortable and there's minimal rainfall. March to May can be hot, while June to October is the rainy season with occasional heavy downpours.

We recommend at least 4-5 days to experience the highlights of Chiang Mai, including temples, local markets, cultural activities, and a day trip to nearby attractions. For a more relaxed pace and deeper exploration, 7-10 days is ideal.

Yes, Chiang Mai is generally very safe for solo travelers, including women. The city has low crime rates, friendly locals, and a well-developed tourism infrastructure. However, always use common sense and standard travel precautions.

Pack light, breathable clothing for warm weather, comfortable walking shoes, a light jacket for air-conditioned spaces, modest clothing for temples (covering shoulders and knees), sunscreen, insect repellent, and a good travel insurance policy.
